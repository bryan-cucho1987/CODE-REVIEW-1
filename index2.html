Ceviche


Your recipe of the month


Peru's National dish, a new trend in the world cuisine scene.


Ceviche is a popular Latin American dish made by macerating raw fish and seafood in an acidic liquid like citrus juice along with ingredients such as chiles, onions, and herbs. The acid in the marinade denatures the proteins in the fish, causing it to become opaque and producing a firm, flaky texture, similar to when it's cooked with heat. Different countries in South and Central America have different spellings for the word, with seviche or cebiche being some of the most common variants. And each country puts its unique spin on the dish, whether by using different fish or seafood or by what sorts of ingredients are used to prepare the marinade.
